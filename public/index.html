📜 The Dharma-Yuddha

Two armies face each other on a sacred field. The Pandavas, led by truth and Krishna's wisdom. The Kauravas, ruled by ambition and Bhishma's iron resolve. The maze that lies between them is no ordinary thing — it is a vyuha, a battle formation drawn from Mahabharat itself.

🌀 The Four Vyuhas

Chakravyuha — the spiral wheel arranged by Drona on Day 13. Six concentric rings, each gap rotated. Abhimanyu broke six before he fell.
Padmavyuha — the lotus formation. Petals radiate outward; only Arjuna and Krishna fully knew how to enter and exit.
Garudavyuha — the eagle formation, named for Vishnu's mount. Wide wings, killing beak at the spearhead.
Makaravyuha — the crocodile formation. Long, narrow, with a crushing jaw at the head.

⚔️ How a round unfolds

  1. Setup phase (60 seconds). The defending team sees the vyuha and clicks tiles to place hidden spike traps (max 4 each). Attackers see only the walls.
  2. Break phase (3 minutes). The attacking team spawns at the green ENTRY. They must reach the gold EXIT alive. Even one survivor reaching the exit means victory.
  3. Round ends when an attacker touches the exit, all attackers fall, or the timer expires.
  4. Teams swap attacker/defender roles after each round. First to 3 wins claims the war.

🧙 Astras — divine weapons

Each warrior carries a divine astra unlocked by deeds: kills, time alive, damage taken, allies remaining. When the seal breaks, press Q to unleash. Some astras may only be used once per match — choose your moment.

  • Pashupatastra (Arjuna) — Shiva's piercing kill-beam.
  • Vasavi Shakti (Karna) — Indra's spear, single-use, instant kill.
  • Vishwarupa (Krishna) — cosmic form, mass stun in awe.
  • Icchamrityu (Bhishma) — cannot die for 8 seconds.
  • Narayanastra (Ashwatthama) — arrow rain when his side is broken.
  • and many more…

🏆 Winning

Best of 5 rounds. Side that wins three rounds first wins the war. Even one survivor in the maze wins the round — the Mahabharat is won not by numbers but by who is left standing.
